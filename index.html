Who am I?

My name is Dante Proto. I was born December 6th, 1994 in Mesa, AZ. I currently live in Queen Creek, AZ, where I grew up. In my younger years, I attended St. Timothy's Catholic Academy 1st through 6th grade. I went to a public school for the first time in the 7th grade at Payne Jr. High. It was around this time when I started playing my favorite sport, American Football. After football season, I became a wrestler. My Jr. High sports career prepared me for High School level competition. I had a very successful Freshman football season. I moved up to the Junior Varsity level, which ended up being where my football career ended. I do miss the field, but football politics ruined the game for me. I did wrestle all 4 years of high school, although I was injury prone my first 3 years in highschool. I did have a very good varsity comeback my senior year, and barely missed my chance at going to the state wrestling tournament(by 3 points). Sports taught me a lot about discipline, work ethic, and how to be a part of a team.

After highschool, I wanted to be a mechanical engineer due to inspiration from my neighbor. I quickly realized that calculus was a big part of engineering, and it was my least favorite class in college. I switced my major to General Business after my first year of college. I have been working towards becoming an accountant. I have not obtained a degree in business yet. However, I did study part time for 3 years while working fast food at Panda Express, and later working retail at Costco. I was unable to promote to a full time position at Costco due to seniority, however, I did realize I enjoyed learning about new technology. I was a sales representative in the electronics section before I left Costco to drive Uber and Lyft full time. It was fun for awhile, however, the hours are hard, and the wear and tear on your vehicle really adds up over time. I landed a job with Dish Network as a Customer Service representative. I handled customer service calls until I was later promoted to technical support. I then handled a mix of tech and customer service calls. As delightful as phone calls may be, I decided that they were not for me, and I was thinking about quitting Dish to do something else before Chat Support came to our site. I was a part of the first 2 chat teams that were created at the Phoenix location. I now handle a mix of tech and customer service chats. I enjoy chat support a lot more than phone calls, as it seems to be more efficient, and I can be more descriptive to help the customer to the best of my ability.

The only problem with my job at Dish is there is not a whole lot of opportunity to advance through chat. That is why I am currently attending UA Coding Bootcamp, to obtain my certificate in Full Stack Web Development. I love writing code, and have worked hard on this page to display my new skills. I have used Bootstrap, and my own CSS on this page. I have added some animations using a cool website called Animista. Feel free to check out my GitHub page to see more of my work. I hope to find a new career in web development before or right after graduating this course. I am very excited to start a new career, and continue learning more and more about web development on the front end and back end side.
